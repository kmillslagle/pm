Address: the close voting split and deadlock risk; deadlock-breaking mechanisms; what matters require cross-block approval; the interaction between block voting and the existing majority-of-Haddock-family-members requirement (Board summary document).
 
3. **Director Succession — Self-Nomination Mechanism:** Design and draft an amendment creating a process for directors to nominate their own successors, including: who can be nominated; what approvals are needed; the non-inheritability of Senior Director status (Edward E. 4. **Amendments Committee Independence Requirement (§5.04(b)):** All proposed OA amendments must pass through the Amendments Committee, which requires a majority of Independent Persons. Analyze whether the proposed dual-block and succession amendments could trigger Independence Requirement issues, and develop a procedural roadmap for getting amendments through the Committee.
 
5. **Senior Director Status — Structural Analysis:** Analyze the implications of Senior Director status being non-inheritable and non-transferable. What happens to board composition and voting dynamics when Senior Directors are no longer serving? Does the OA adequately address this transition?
 
6. **Comprehensive OA Audit:** Conduct a full review of the Operating Agreement for: internal inconsistencies; ambiguities in defined terms; gaps in committee authority definitions; conflicts between the OA and the committee role summary documents; provisions that may be inconsistent with Chapter 662, Florida Statutes.
 
7. **Committee Structure Review:** Review the four committee structures (Board, Amendments Committee, Investment Committee, Discretionary Distributions Committee) for: overlapping authority; gaps in oversight; adequacy of the DDC's delegation provisions (§5.02(c)); Investment Committee's relationship to SunGate Capital; whether the Standing Investment Committee concept (§5.03) is being properly utilized.

9. **Dissolution Provisions (Article XI):** Review §11.01 and §11.02 for adequacy. The dissolution trigger requires Board approval plus Members holding 75% of outstanding units. Assess whether this threshold is appropriate and whether the liquidation procedures are complete.
 
10. **Indemnification and Liability (Article X):** Review §§10.01–10.08 for: scope of Company Agent definition; adequacy of indemnification; interaction with fiduciary duties under Chapter 662; whether D&O insurance provisions (§10.05) are adequate for an unlicensed family trust company.

### WORKSTREAM 2: Tax Structure & Fee Deductibility
 
The goal is to maximize the ability to deduct trust company expenses. The critical architectural decision is a non-grantor trust ownership structure, which avoids a circularity problem that would defeat above-the-line deductibility. The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA, signed July 4, 2025) permanently eliminated miscellaneous itemized deductions, making IRC §67(e) qualification essential.
 
**Generate cards for each of these:**
 
1. **Non-Grantor Trust Formation:** Draft or review the instrument creating the new non-grantor trust that will own FFTC. Ensure grantor trust powers (IRC §§671–679) are excluded. Confirm that ownership by a non-grantor trust enables the trust to claim above-the-line deductions for trust company fees under §67(e).
 
2. **IRC §67(e) Compliance Framework:** Develop a framework for ensuring all FFTC fees and charges to client trusts qualify as "costs which are paid or incurred in connection with the administration of the trust and which would not have been incurred if the property were not held in such trust." Apply the Knight v. Commissioner (2008) standard and Treasury Regulation §1.67-4.
 
3. **Fee Structure Design — Base Fees:** Design a fee schedule for FFTC's services to client trusts that maximizes §67(e) qualification. Fees must be structured so they are "unique to trusts" — i.e., costs an individual would not incur for the same property. Include: trustee compensation, fiduciary accounting, tax return preparation, trust administration, distribution committee services, trust instrument interpretation.
 
4. **Performance-Contingent Fee Structure:** Design and draft a performance-contingent fee component tied to fiduciary benchmarks (analogous to carried interest). This is a vehicle for above-the-line deductibility because performance-based fees tied to fiduciary obligations are inherently trust-specific. Define: benchmarks, measurement periods, calculation methodology, documentation requirements.
 
5. **OBBBA Impact Analysis:** Analyze the impact of the One Big Beautiful Bill Act's permanent repeal of miscellaneous itemized deductions. Confirm that the below-the-line path is permanently foreclosed and that all fee structures must qualify under §67(e) or another above-the-line provision.
 
6. **Tax Structure Planning Memorandum — Final Version:** Produce a final version of the Tax Structure Planning Memorandum incorporating: the non-grantor trust ownership decision; §67(e) analysis; fee structure recommendations; OBBBA impact; risk analysis; implementation timeline.
 
7. **Ownership Transition Plan:** Develop a plan for transitioning FFTC ownership from the current Member (Family Trust 2) to the new non-grantor trust. Address: membership unit transfer under Article IX of the OA; Board approval requirements; tax consequences of the transfer; timing and sequencing.
 
8. **Investment Advisory Fee Analysis:** Analyze the deductibility of investment advisory fees (SunGate Capital) under the new structure. Under Knight, investment advisory fees are generally not unique to trusts unless the investment mandate is fiduciary in nature. Determine whether SunGate's engagement structure can be framed to qualify under §67(e).
